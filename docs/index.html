The charts

02 — Visuals

Monthly temperature climatology

Average max, mean, and min temperature by calendar month — May is the hottest month, slightly ahead of June, with monsoon clouds (Jul–Sep) bringing temperatures down a few degrees before winter sets in.

Yearly temperature trend

A gradual warming trend across the decade, though year-to-year noise is larger than the trend itself.

Annual rainfall, year by year

Some years cleared 1700mm; others stayed below 1000mm — a reminder of how unpredictable a single monsoon season can be.

Where the rain actually falls

June through September alone account for the overwhelming majority of the city's annual rainfall.

Year × month heatmap

Every month, every year, at a glance — useful for spotting subtle warming creeping into specific months over time.

Temperature vs. rainfall, day by day

Each dot is one day, colored by month. Rain clusters at moderate-to-warm temperatures during monsoon months (orange/pink) — confirming the weak overall correlation hides a strong seasonal pattern.

How it was built

03 — Method
  • Data: 10 years of daily temperature (max/min/mean) and rainfall pulled from the free Open-Meteo Historical Weather API — no API key required.
  • Cleaning: Missing temperature readings interpolated using time-aware methods; missing rainfall treated as zero (the more defensible assumption for a highly non-smooth variable).
  • Analysis: Yearly and monthly aggregation with Pandas, linear trend fitting with NumPy, and a correlation check between daily temperature and rainfall.
  • Visualization: All charts built with Matplotlib — a trend line, a climatology curve, a monsoon-highlighted bar chart, a year × month heatmap, and a colored scatter plot.
